# Traffic Volume Analysis and Operational Performance Assessment of Chandrasekharpur Intersection, Bhubaneswar

## Project Overview
This project presents a transportation engineering study of the Chandrasekharpur Intersection located in Bhubaneswar, Odisha. The objective is to evaluate the operational performance of the intersection under mixed traffic conditions using traffic engineering principles and IRC-based methodologies.

The study focuses on the analysis of traffic flow characteristics, capacity utilization, and service quality through the computation of key performance indicators such as Traffic Composition, Peak Hour Factor (PHF), Saturation Flow Rate, Volume-to-Capacity Ratio (V/C), and Level of Service (LOS).

## Objectives
- Analyze classified traffic volume data for a major urban intersection.
- Evaluate traffic composition under heterogeneous traffic conditions.
- Study turning movement distribution and traffic demand characteristics.
- Calculate Peak Hour Factor (PHF) and Saturation Flow Rate.
- Determine Volume-to-Capacity (V/C) Ratio and Level of Service (LOS).
- Assess operational efficiency and identify congestion trends affecting intersection performance.

## Study Location
Chandrasekharpur Intersection 
Bhubaneswar, Odisha, Idia

The selectecd intersection experiences significant traffic demand due to residential, commercial and institutional developments in the surrounding area, making it suitable for operational performance assessment.

## Methodology
The project follows the following workflow:
1. Collection of classified traffic volume data.
2. Vehicle categorization into:Cars,Two-Wheelers,Three-Wheelers,SUVs,Light Commercial Vehicles (LCV) and Heavy Commercial Vehicles (HCV)
3. Traffic composition analysis.
4. Turning movement analysis.
5. Passenger Car Unit (PCU) conversion using IRC equivalency factors.
6. Peak Hour Volume (PHV) and Peak Hour Factor (PHF) calculations.
7. Saturation Flow Rate estimation.
8. Volume-to-Capacity (V/C) Ratio computation.
9. Level of Service (LOS) determination.
10. Interpretation of operational performance and congestion conditions.

## Tools used
Microsoft Excel ,Transportation engineering principles, IRC Guidelines

## Preliminary Findings
The analysis indicates that two-wheelers and cars constitute the dominant share of traffic at the selected intersection. Capacity utilization and operational performance are assessed using V/C Ratio and LOS evaluation techniques to identify potential congestion and operational deficiencies.
